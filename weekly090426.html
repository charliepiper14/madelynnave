The Core Problem This Week: Revenue Collapsed, Spend Held

Purchases ticked up slightly from 22 to 23 but revenue dropped 48% from $33,601 to $17,442. That gap is entirely explained by AOV compression: prior week AOV was approximately $1,527 (driven by a $13,211 single-order conversion on the Carousel ad and a $6,821 order on the TLIMS Brand Logo). This week AOV is $758, which sits closer to the real account average. The prior week numbers contained attribution anomalies that inflated the ROAS picture to 3.88x. The real baseline is closer to what we're seeing now: 2.16x blended on $8K spend.

What the Numbers Actually Mean

The TLIMS CBO is the biggest concern this week. The core Value Optimisation ad set delivered zero purchases on $950 in spend, while the Easter/Spring ad set and Eden Collection are carrying the campaign's entire weight. Fine Art ABO bounced back strongly to 2.97x ROAS. Combined test is delivering solid returns driven entirely by the Passage Video format. The account is running at $1,154/day average, spread across ad sets with wildly inconsistent efficiency. The cleanup work done in March has partially held but there are active waste vectors that need addressing now.
