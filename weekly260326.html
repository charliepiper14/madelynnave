Critical: Performance Declined This Week

Spend increased 62.5% week over week but purchases dropped from 23 to 22 and CPA jumped from $300 to $509. ROAS compressed from 1.32x to 1.25x. The spend increase is driven by Easter creative ramping across multiple ad sets, newly activated ad sets (Broad Winter Collection 2nd, Value Optimisation 2nd), and expanded Combined test Easter ads. Much of this new spend is delivering zero or near-zero returns. The account is now spending more than double the prior audit period's daily rate with significantly worse efficiency.

What Changed Since the Call (Mar 20)

On the call with Oscar we aligned on pausing all zero-purchase ads, consolidating Value Optimisation ad sets, and concentrating Easter budget on A Hill Far Away. The data this week confirms the urgency of those actions. The second Value Optimisation ad set is spending at $756 CPA. The second Broad Winter Collection is spending at $512 CPA. Easter Grid ads are consuming budget at 0.00x ROAS. Every day without these pauses is incremental waste.

Oscar's Reported Revenue Discrepancy

On the call, Oscar flagged that the business saw approximately $30,000 less revenue than Meta reported in a comparable window. Conversion rate also dropped 50% while Meta showed traffic increasing. This suggests Meta is over-attributing conversions and revenue, meaning actual ROAS is likely lower than the figures in this report.

  • Pixel audit initiated with Avashesh (Avi)
  • Checking for duplicate event firing
  • UTM and parameter setup under review
  • Triple Whale paid tier recommended for cross-attribution

What This Means for the Numbers

If Meta is over-reporting by even 20 to 30%, the true ROAS figures this week are closer to 0.88x to 1.00x blended, and the true monthly ROAS is closer to 0.84x to 0.96x. That would put the account in loss-making territory for March overall.

  • At 20% over-reporting: true weekly ROAS is approximately 1.00x
  • At 30% over-reporting: true weekly ROAS is approximately 0.88x
  • CPA could be higher than $509 in reality
  • Avi's audit results expected within 1 to 2 weeks

Action: Do Not Wait for the Audit to Act

The attribution discrepancy makes the waste problem more urgent, not less. Even taking Meta's numbers at face value, the account is spending 55% of budget on zero or negative return ads. If Meta is over-attributing, the true waste is even higher. The pauses and consolidation agreed on the call should be executed immediately regardless of audit timing. Oscar has given full budget flexibility to make these changes.
