Q1 Delivered Less Revenue on More Spend

The account spent $7,027 more in Q1 than Q4 but generated $24,091 less in revenue. ROAS compressed from 2.00x to 1.66x, a 17% efficiency decline. CPA rose from $338 to $377, moving further from the $150-$250 target range. The quarter was characterised by campaign proliferation, creative fatigue on legacy winners, and ad sets running unprofitably for extended periods before being paused.

Structural Shifts in Q1

Q1 introduced the Combined Christian + Fine Art test campaign, expanded Easter/Spring creative, and launched the Eden Collection (Spring '26) ad set. Several underperforming ad sets (Broad Winter Collection, Advantage+ Broad, Broad 15th October, LLA, Stacked Interest) have since been paused. The account has been significantly consolidated heading into Q2, with active spend now concentrated in just five ad sets across three campaigns.
