Q1 Delivered Less Revenue on More Spend

The account spent $7,027 more in Q1 than Q4 but generated $24,091 less in revenue. That incremental spend produced a negative return. ROAS compressed from 2.00x to 1.66x, a 17% efficiency decline. CPA rose from $338 to $377, moving further from the $150-$250 target range. The quarter was characterised by campaign proliferation, creative fatigue on legacy winners, and insufficient performance management of underperforming ads and ad sets.

Structural Shifts in Q1

Q1 introduced the Combined Christian + Fine Art test campaign, expanded Easter/Spring creative across multiple ad sets, and launched the Eden Collection (Spring '26) ad set. The Fine Art ABO campaign shifted from Advantage+ Broad dominance in Q4 toward Value Optimisation as the primary spend vehicle. AOV also compressed, with average order values declining across campaigns as lower-priced products entered the mix.

Strong Launch: Best New Ad Set This Quarter

3.86x ROAS at $211 CPA in the first 3 days is the strongest launch performance of any ad set this quarter. The Eden static and video creatives are converting immediately, which is unusual for a brand new ad set in the learning phase. 36 adds to cart from $846 of spend ($23.49 per ATC) shows strong product-market resonance. The ad set is delivering above its $250/day budget at $282/day average, indicating Meta's algorithm sees positive signals and is pushing spend.

Learning Phase Context

This ad set needs roughly 50 conversions in 7 days to exit the learning phase (Meta's standard for Value Optimisation). With 4 purchases in 3 days, the conversion velocity is below that threshold. However, at this AOV ($530+ average), 50 purchases in a week at current budget is unrealistic. The more relevant signal is the cost per add to cart ($23.49) and the purchase conversion rate. Do not increase budget beyond $250/day for the first 7 days. Let the algorithm stabilise. After 7 days, if ROAS holds above 2x, scale to $300-$350/day following the 20% weekly rule.
